 

# **📘 STORY OVERVIEW**

## **Narrative Summary**

The game follows a man whose mind is fracturing after a violent incident involving his partner. Following a rejected
marriage proposal, he spiraled into obsession and rage, ultimately killing her. Unable to face what he has done, his
psyche constructs an alternate reality inside his apartment. The answering machine becomes the manifestation of his
guilt — replaying distorted fragments of the truth as he moves through the space.

Each room represents a reconstructed memory. Each message escalates the emotional and psychological pressure. As the
player progresses, the apartment shifts from normal to increasingly surreal, revealing the truth piece by piece. The
final sequence exposes the protagonist’s crime and collapses the illusion entirely.

---

# **🦴 Narrative Spine (Emotional Core)**

The story is built around the protagonist’s emotional descent:

- **Obsession** over the relationship
- **Rejection** when his proposal is refused
- **Violence** triggered by emotional instability
- **Guilt** buried beneath denial
- **Psychological Collapse** as the truth resurfaces

The game’s horror is rooted in **internal conflict**, not external threats.

---

# **🔑 Core Secret**

The answering machine is not a real device.  
It is the protagonist’s **fractured mind**, replaying distorted memories and accusations.

- Early messages sound normal and human.
- Mid‑game messages become distorted and accusatory.
- The final message is his own voice, confessing the truth.

The supernatural elements (objects appearing, rooms changing, impossible reflections) represent his subconscious
reconstructing the crime he tried to suppress.

---

# **📞 Story Progression (6‑Beat Sequence)**

Each beat corresponds to a room, a message, and an environmental shift.

---

## **Beat 1 — Hallway: “It was just a fight.”**

**Message Tone:** Calm, apologetic.  
**Message Intent:** A normal voicemail from her, left earlier that day.  
**Environment:**

- Apartment appears normal.
- Closed ring box on the floor.

**Purpose:** Establishes a breakup narrative and misleads the player.

---

## **Beat 2 — Kitchen: “You were angry.”**

**Message Tone:** Strained, emotional.  
**Message Intent:** She references the argument and his temper.  
**Environment:**

- Broken picture frame
- Dish towel with faint stain
- Two wine glasses

**Purpose:** Introduces tension and subtle signs of violence.

---

## **Beat 3 — Bedroom: “You didn’t let me leave.”**

**Message Tone:** Distorted, glitching.  
**Message Intent:** She describes him grabbing her and blocking her exit.  
**Environment:**

- Her shoe under the bed
- Half‑open suitcase
- Ring box now open and empty

**Purpose:** Supernatural elements begin; memory reconstruction intensifies.

---

## **Beat 4 — Bathroom: “You hurt me.”**

**Message Tone:** Layered, accusatory, almost inhuman.  
**Message Intent:** She confronts him about the moment things turned violent.  
**Environment:**

- Flickering mirror
- Wet handprint on shower glass
- Her hair tie on the floor

**Purpose:** The apartment contradicts his denial; guilt surfaces.

---

## **Beat 5 — Living Room: “You dragged me.”**

**Message Tone:** Distorted beyond recognition.  
**Message Intent:** She recounts being pulled back inside after trying to leave.  
**Environment:**

- Overturned furniture
- Broken lamp
- Faint trail leading to the final door
- Answering machine light flickering erratically

**Purpose:** The truth is nearly complete; tension peaks.

---

## **Beat 6 — Final Room: “You killed me.”**

**Message Tone:** His own voice, calm and cold.  
**Message Intent:** A full confession — the answering machine was his mind.  
**Environment Transition:**

- Bright flash
- Apartment dissolves into an infinite void
- Wooden floor stretching into darkness
- A woman’s body lies motionless on the ground
- A black puddle beneath her
- A knife on the floor beside her
- Player’s hands are empty
- Locomotion disabled

**Purpose:** The illusion collapses. The player confronts the truth.

**Ending:**  
A single answering‑machine beep echoes.  
Slow fade to black.  
**The End.**
